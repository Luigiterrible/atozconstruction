What Businesses Should Know Before a Tenant Improvement Project

Tenant improvements move better when layout, systems, landlord coordination, operations, and timeline pressure are sorted before construction starts.

Commercial5 min readTenant Improvements

Tenant improvement projects can look straightforward from the outside, but they usually involve more coordination than people expect. Business needs, building rules, utility capacity, finish level, landlord approvals, and occupancy timelines all need to stay aligned.

Start With Function and Flow

The layout should support how the business actually operates, not just how the space looks on paper.

Review Systems Early

Power, HVAC, plumbing, lighting, data, and existing conditions influence TI scope more than many teams expect.

Coordinate Timeline With Operations

Move-in targets, opening dates, staffing plans, and disruption tolerance all affect the right sequencing strategy.

What Usually Creates Surprises?

Permit timing, landlord review, building access rules, limited utility capacity, existing-condition issues, and last-minute finish changes are common sources of project friction. The earlier these are surfaced, the smoother the project tends to move.

Contractor Tip

The best TI projects start with a clear business plan, not just a construction plan.

How to Plan a Smarter TI Project

Clarify goals, systems, approvals, finish expectations, and occupancy needs before field work ramps up. That gives your team more control over both budget and timing.
